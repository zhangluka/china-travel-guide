# VPN Guide for China

**Priority: 🔴 Set up BEFORE your trip**

China's "Great Firewall" blocks access to:
- **Google** (Search, Maps, Gmail, Translate, YouTube)
- **Social media** (Instagram, Facebook, X/Twitter, Snapchat)
- **Messaging** (WhatsApp, Telegram, Line, Signal)
- **News** (BBC, NY Times, Reuters, most Western news)
- **Other** (Wikipedia, Reddit, most Google services)

**Without a VPN, you cannot access any of these sites from within China.**

## What is a VPN?

A VPN (Virtual Private Network) encrypts your internet traffic and routes it through a server outside China, bypassing the Great Firewall. It makes it appear as if you're browsing from another country.

## How to Set Up

### Before You Leave Home

1. **Subscribe to a VPN** (we recommend Astrill or LetsVPN)
2. **Download and install the app** on ALL your devices (phone, laptop, tablet)
3. **Log in and test** that it works from your home country
4. **Download the VPN's manual configuration files** as backup
5. **Save the VPN's support email/chat link** in case you need help

> 🔴 **Critical**: You CANNOT download VPN apps from within China. App stores (Google Play) are blocked. You must install before arriving.

### On Arrival

1. Connect to the VPN immediately after landing
2. Choose a server in a nearby country (Japan, Singapore, Hong Kong, or US West Coast)
3. Test that Google and WhatsApp work
4. Keep the VPN running throughout your trip

## eSIM Trick: Bypass the Firewall

Some eSIM providers route traffic through Hong Kong or other locations outside the Great Firewall, meaning **you don't need a VPN at all**:

- **3HK eSIM** (Hong Kong SIM): Routes through HK, no firewall restrictions
- **Airalo eSIM**: Some plans bypass the firewall
- **Nomad eSIM**: Check if the plan routes through a non-China location

> 💡 **Pro tip**: A Hong Kong eSIM is the easiest solution — no VPN needed, and you get decent data speeds.

## Common Problems

**"VPN connected but nothing loads"**: Try switching servers. China blocks specific IP ranges, so one server may be down while others work.

**"VPN keeps disconnecting"**: This is normal. China actively disrupts VPN connections. Reconnect and try a different protocol.

**"Can't download the VPN app"**: If you forgot to pre-install, try:
1. Use a friend's phone hotspot (if they have a non-China connection)
2. Ask your hotel concierge for help
3. Use the VPN provider's website to download directly (if it's not blocked)

## What You CAN Access Without VPN

These work fine in China without a VPN:
- WeChat, Alipay, Taobao, JD.com (Chinese apps)
- Apple services (iMessage, iCloud, App Store)
- Most hotel Wi-Fi login pages
- Your email if it's not Gmail (Outlook, Yahoo usually work)
