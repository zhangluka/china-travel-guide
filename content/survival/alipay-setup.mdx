# How to Set Up Alipay as a Foreigner

**Priority: 🔴 Do this BEFORE your trip**

China is essentially a cashless society. Even street food vendors, taxi drivers, and convenience stores expect mobile payment. Without Alipay or WeChat Pay, you will struggle to pay for almost anything.

The good news: Alipay now supports foreign credit cards. Here's exactly how to set it up.

## Step 1: Download Alipay

- **iOS**: Search "Alipay" in the App Store (支付宝)
- **Android**: Download from Google Play or the Alipay website

> ⚠️ **Important**: Download and set up Alipay BEFORE entering China. The Google Play Store is blocked in China (you'd need a VPN to access it).

## Step 2: Register with Your Phone Number

1. Open Alipay and tap "Sign Up"
2. Enter your **international phone number** (with country code)
3. You'll receive an SMS verification code
4. Set a login password

> 💡 **Tip**: You can use your home country phone number. You don't need a Chinese SIM card for basic Alipay functionality.

## Step 3: Complete Identity Verification

1. Go to "Me" → "Settings" → "Identity Verification"
2. Select "Foreign Passport" as your ID type
3. Enter your passport number and full name (must match your passport exactly)
4. You may need to take a photo of your passport

## Step 4: Link Your Foreign Credit Card

1. Go to "Me" → "Bank Cards" → "Add Card"
2. Enter your international credit card details
   - Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Diners Club, and Discover are supported
   - American Express has limited support
3. Your bank will send a verification code
4. Once verified, the card appears in your Alipay wallet

## Step 5: Set Up Payment PIN

1. Go to "Me" → "Settings" → "Payment Settings"
2. Set a 6-digit payment PIN
3. This PIN is required for every transaction

## How to Pay

When you need to pay:

1. **To pay a merchant**: Open Alipay → tap "Pay" → show the QR code to the merchant's scanner
2. **To scan a merchant**: Open Alipay → tap "Scan" → scan the merchant's QR code → enter amount → confirm

## Common Issues

**"Transaction failed"**: Your bank may block international transactions. Call your bank before traveling to authorize China transactions.

**"Identity verification failed"**: Make sure your name matches your passport exactly. Middle names matter.

**"Card not supported"**: Try a different card. Visa and Mastercard have the highest success rate.

## Pro Tips

- **Always have Alipay AND WeChat Pay set up** — some merchants only accept one
- **Keep some cash as backup** — ¥500-1000 in small bills for emergencies
- **Alipay mini-programs** let you book taxis (Didi), order food (Meituan), buy train tickets (12306), and more — all within the Alipay app
- **Enable notifications** so you can see payment confirmations instantly
