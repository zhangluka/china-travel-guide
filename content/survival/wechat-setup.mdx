# 💬 How to Use WeChat in China

WeChat is China's super-app — messaging, payments, food delivery, ride-hailing, and more, all in one app. If Alipay is your wallet, WeChat is your lifeline.

## What Is WeChat?

**WeChat** (微信, Wēixìn) is used by over 1 billion people in China. It's not just a messaging app — it's an entire digital ecosystem:

- 💬 **Messaging** — text, voice, video calls
- 💰 **WeChat Pay** — mobile payments (QR codes)
- 🚗 **Ride-hailing** — Didi integration
- 🍜 **Food delivery** — Meituan integration
- 🎫 **Mini programs** — thousands of apps-within-an-app
- 🌐 **Social media** — Moments (like Facebook feed)
- 🔍 **Search** — find restaurants, attractions, services

## Do I Need WeChat AND Alipay?

**Yes, ideally.** Here's why:

| | WeChat | Alipay |
|---|---|---|
| **Messaging** | ✅ Primary use | ❌ Not a messaging app |
| **Payments** | ✅ Works everywhere | ✅ Works everywhere |
| **Mini programs** | ✅ More social/local | ✅ More travel/booking |
| **Taxi/ride-hailing** | ✅ Didi built-in | ✅ Didi built-in |
| **Foreign cards** | ✅ Supported | ✅ Supported |
| **English interface** | ⚠️ Partial | ✅ Better |

**Bottom line:** Set up both. Alipay for payments and travel services, WeChat for messaging and local life. Some places only accept one or the other.

## How to Set Up WeChat

### Step 1: Download
- **iOS**: App Store → search "WeChat"
- **Android**: Google Play → search "WeChat" (or download APK from wechat.com if Play Store is blocked)

### Step 2: Register
1. Open WeChat → "Sign Up"
2. Enter your phone number (any country)
3. Verify via SMS code
4. Set a display name and password
5. Done — you're in

### Step 3: Set Up WeChat Pay
1. Go to **Me** → **Services** → **Wallet**
2. Tap **Add Card** (银行卡)
3. Enter your passport info when prompted
4. Add your foreign credit/debit card (Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Discover supported)
5. Verify via SMS from your bank
6. Done — you can now pay with QR codes

> ⚠️ **Important**: WeChat Pay with foreign cards has limits:
> - Single transaction: ¥200 (can be increased to ¥5,000 with verification)
> - Daily total: ¥6,000
> - Annual total: ¥60,000
> - Some small merchants may not accept foreign cards

### Step 4: Enable English
1. Go to **Me** → **Settings** → **General** → **Language**
2. Select **English**
3. Note: Many mini programs and official accounts are still in Chinese

## Essential WeChat Features for Tourists

### 💰 WeChat Pay (Paying with QR Codes)
How it works:
1. Open WeChat → tap **+** → **Money** (付款)
2. Show your QR code to the merchant
3. They scan it → payment done

Or scan the merchant's QR code:
1. Open WeChat → tap **+** → **Scan** (扫一扫)
2. Scan the merchant's QR code
3. Enter amount → confirm

### 🚗 Didi (Ride-Hailing)
WeChat has Didi built in:
1. Open WeChat → **Discover** → **Mini Programs**
2. Search "Didi" (滴滴出行)
3. Or: tap **+** → **Scan** → scan a Didi QR code
4. Book rides, pay through WeChat

### 🍜 Food Delivery (Meituan)
1. Open WeChat → **Discover** → **Mini Programs**
2. Search "Meituan" (美团)
3. Order food, pay through WeChat

### 🌐 Translation
WeChat has a built-in translator:
1. Long-press any message → **Translate**
2. Or: **Discover** → **Scan** → point camera at Chinese text → instant translation

### 📍 Location Sharing
Share your real-time location with friends:
1. In a chat → tap **+** → **Location**
2. Choose **Share Live Location**
3. Useful when meeting up or if lost

### 🎫 Mini Programs (小程序)
Think of these as apps-within-WeChat. Useful ones for tourists:

| Mini Program | What It Does |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Didi** (滴滴出行) | Ride-hailing |
| **Meituan** (美团) | Food delivery, restaurant reviews |
| **Ctrip/Trip.com** (携程) | Train/hotel booking |
| **Dianping** (大众点评) | Restaurant reviews (like Yelp) |
| **Metro** (各地地铁) | City metro maps and QR tickets |
| **Translation** (翻译) | Text/camera translation |

To find mini programs: **Discover** → **Mini Programs** → search by Chinese name

### 💬 Messaging
Add friends by:
- **WeChat ID**: Ask for their ID, search and add
- **QR code**: Scan their personal QR code
- **Phone number**: If they're in your contacts

## Pro Tips

1. **Set up WeChat Pay before you arrive** — add your foreign card while you still have easy access to SMS verification from your bank.

2. **WeChat Pay works almost everywhere** — street vendors, metro, taxis, restaurants, convenience stores. If they have a QR code, you can pay.

3. **Keep some cash as backup** — very small vendors in rural areas might not accept foreign WeChat Pay.

4. **The translate feature is a lifesaver** — long-press any Chinese text in WeChat to translate it instantly.

5. **Join local WeChat groups** — ask your hotel or hostel for their guest group. Great for tips and meetups.

6. **WeChat Moments is China's social media** — if you want to share your trip with Chinese friends, post to Moments.

7. **Voice messages are common in China** — don't be surprised if people send voice clips instead of text.

8. **Mini programs are how locals use services** — instead of downloading separate apps, Chinese users use mini programs within WeChat.

## WeChat vs Alipay: Which to Prioritize?

| Use Case | Better Option |
|----------|--------------|
| Paying at restaurants | Both work equally |
| Paying street vendors | Both work equally |
| Booking trains | Alipay (Trip.com integration) |
| Ordering food delivery | WeChat (Meituan) |
| Calling a taxi | Both (Didi in both) |
| Messaging locals | WeChat (everyone uses it) |
| Translation | Both have it, Alipay slightly better |
| Mini programs | WeChat has more variety |

**Recommendation:** Set up Alipay first (better for tourists), then WeChat (essential for communication). You'll end up using both.

## FAQ

**Is WeChat free to use?**
Yes, WeChat is free to download and use. Messaging, voice calls, and video calls are free. WeChat Pay has no fees for normal transactions.

**Can I use WeChat without a Chinese phone number?**
Yes, you can register with any international phone number. However, some features (like certain mini programs) may require a Chinese number.

**Is WeChat safe to link my credit card?**
WeChat is owned by Tencent, one of China's largest tech companies. It uses encryption and security measures. Millions of foreigners use it. It's generally considered safe.

**What if my WeChat Pay doesn't work?**
Common issues: card not supported (try a different card), transaction limit reached, or merchant doesn't accept foreign cards. Carry some cash as backup.

**Can I video call home using WeChat?**
Yes, WeChat supports free voice and video calls to other WeChat users worldwide. However, you'll need a VPN to use it alongside WhatsApp/Zoom.

**Do I need WeChat if I already have Alipay?**
For payments, Alipay is sufficient. But WeChat is essential for communicating with locals, joining groups, and using social features that Alipay doesn't have.
