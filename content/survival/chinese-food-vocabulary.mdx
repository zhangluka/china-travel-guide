# 🍜 Chinese Food Vocabulary

Chinese menus are usually in Chinese. Here's your cheat sheet for ordering food, understanding what you're eating, and avoiding things you don't want.

## The Essential Ordering Phrase

**我要这个 (Wǒ yào zhège)** — "I want this one"

Point at the menu item, the dish on another table, or the picture. Say this phrase. Done.

## How to Order

### At a Restaurant with Menu
1. **Sit down** — wait to be seated or just find a table
2. **Get the menu** — say "菜单" (cài dān) or point at one
3. **Order** — say "我要这个" (wǒ yào zhège) while pointing
4. **Confirm quantity** — hold up fingers for how many
5. **Wait** — food comes when it's ready

### At a Street Stall
1. **Look at what others are eating** — point at something similar
2. **Say "我要这个"** (wǒ yào zhège)
3. **Pay** — show Alipay/WeChat QR code
4. **Eat** — usually standing or at a small table

### At a Hotpot Restaurant
1. **Choose broth** — "红锅" (hóng guō, spicy) or "鸳鸯锅" (yuānyāng guō, half-and-half)
2. **Order ingredients** — point at the menu or the display fridge
3. **Cook yourself** — dip items in the boiling broth
4. **Dip in sauce** — sesame oil + garlic is the standard

## Pro Tips

1. **Point at pictures** — most Chinese restaurants have picture menus or photos on the wall. Just point.

2. **Look at other tables** — see something that looks good? Point at it and say "我要这个."

3. **Google Translate camera** — point your camera at the menu for instant translation.

4. **Say "好吃" after eating** — it means "delicious" and makes the staff very happy.

5. **Don't be afraid of unfamiliar dishes** — Chinese food is generally safe and delicious. Try something new.

6. **Ask for "不辣" if you can't handle spice** — "bù là" means "not spicy."

7. **Street food is safe** — millions of locals eat it daily. If there's a line, it's good.

8. **Small portions = more variety** — order several small dishes to share rather than one big one.
