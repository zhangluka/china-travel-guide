## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Eat street food** — it's not just cheap, it's often better than restaurants
2. **Take the metro** — Didi adds up fast. Metro is ¥2-8 per trip.
3. **Book trains early** — prices don't change, but availability does
4. **Travel off-peak** — avoid Chinese holidays (National Day week, Chinese New Year)
5. **Stay near metro stations** — saves on transport costs
6. **Free attractions** — many museums and parks are free
7. **Bring a water bottle** — tap water isn't drinkable, but you can refill at hotels
8. **Use Alipay/WeChat Pay** — no ATM fees, better exchange rates

## FAQ

**How much money do I need for 2 weeks in China?**
For mid-range travel: ¥9,000-14,000 ($1,260-1,960) including accommodation, food, transport, and attractions. Budget travelers can do it for ¥5,000-7,000.

**Is China expensive for tourists?**
No, China is very affordable compared to Japan, Korea, Australia, or Western Europe. Street food costs $2-4, metro rides cost $0.30-1.00, and 3-star hotels cost $35-60/night.

**Should I bring cash to China?**
Bring some USD/EUR to exchange as backup, but plan to use Alipay or WeChat Pay for everything. Cash is rarely used in Chinese cities.

**How much does a China trip cost from the US?**
A 10-day mid-range trip costs about $900-1,200 within China (excluding flights). Round-trip flights from the US to China typically cost $600-1,200.

**Is tipping expected in China?**
No. Tipping is not customary in China and can even cause confusion. Don't tip at restaurants, hotels, or taxis.
