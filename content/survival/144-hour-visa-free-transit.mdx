# 144-Hour Visa-Free Transit China 2025: The Complete Guide

**Last updated: April 2025**

Good news: you might not need a visa to visit China.

China's 144-hour visa-free transit policy lets citizens from **54 countries** stay up to **6 days** without a visa. No embassy visits. No application fees. Just show up with the right documents.

Here's everything you need to know.

---

## What Is the 144-Hour Visa-Free Transit?

It's a policy that allows eligible travelers to transit through China and stay for up to 144 hours (6 days) without a visa. The key word is **transit** — you must be traveling between two different countries.

**Example:** Flying from Tokyo → Shanghai (stay 5 days) → Bangkok ✅

This works because you're transiting through China on your way from Japan to Thailand.

**Doesn't work:** Flying from Tokyo → Shanghai → Tokyo ❌

That's a round trip, not transit.

## What Documents Do You Need?

At the immigration counter, prepare:

1. **Valid passport** (at least 6 months validity)
2. **Onward ticket** to a third country (confirmed booking)
3. **Completed arrival card** (given on plane or at airport)

**Important:** The onward ticket must be to a **different country** than where you came from.

✅ Tokyo → Shanghai → Bangkok (works)
❌ Tokyo → Shanghai → Tokyo (doesn't work)

## Step-by-Step Process

### Before You Fly

1. **Check eligibility** — Confirm your country is on the list
2. **Book onward travel** — You need a confirmed ticket leaving China
3. **Check entry point** — Make sure your airport/port is approved
4. **Print documents** — Have your onward ticket confirmation ready

### At Departure Airport

1. **Check-in counter** — Show your passport and onward ticket
2. **Airline verification** — The airline will verify your eligibility
3. **Boarding** — You'll be allowed to board

### Arriving in China

1. **Fill arrival card** — Complete the entry card (distributed on plane)
2. **Go to immigration** — Look for "144-Hour Visa-Free Transit" counter
3. **Present documents** — Passport + onward ticket + arrival card
4. **Get stamp** — Immigration stamps your passport with exit date
5. **Collect luggage** — Pick up your bags
6. **Exit airport** — You're in!

### During Your Stay

- **Stay within the allowed area** — Don't leave the designated region
- **Keep documents safe** — Carry passport and hotel confirmations
- **Note your exit date** — Count 144 hours from midnight of arrival day

### Leaving China

1. **Arrive early** — Be at airport 3 hours before departure
2. **Immigration exit** — Show passport with entry stamp
3. **Depart** — Fly to your next destination

## Common Questions

### Can I extend beyond 144 hours?

**No.** The 144 hours cannot be extended. You must leave before it expires. Overstaying results in fines and potential entry bans.

### Can I visit multiple cities?

**Yes, but only within the allowed region.** For example:
- Shanghai → Hangzhou → Suzhou ✅ (all in Yangtze Delta)
- Shanghai → Beijing ❌ (different regions)

### Do I need a hotel booking?

**Not required**, but highly recommended. Immigration may ask where you're staying.

### Can I work or study?

**No.** This is strictly for tourism and transit. No work, study, or business activities allowed.

### What if my flight is delayed?

Contact immigration immediately. Delays due to weather or airline issues are generally accommodated, but you must report to immigration.

### Can I enter by land?

**Yes**, at some ports. For example:
- Hong Kong → Shenzhen (by train) ✅
- Macau → Zhuhai (by land) ✅

But you still need an onward ticket to a third country.

## Pro Tips

### 1. Book Refundable Onward Tickets
Some travelers book a refundable ticket to a third country, then cancel after entry. **We don't recommend this** — immigration may verify your booking.

### 2. Use This for Short Business Trips
If you have a 3-day conference in Shanghai, this saves you visa hassle.

### 3. Combine with Other Destinations
Tokyo → Shanghai (4 days) → Hong Kong (3 days) → Home
This gives you China + Hong Kong without a China visa.

### 4. Check Regional Restrictions
You **cannot** leave the designated region. If you enter via Shanghai, you can't visit Beijing.

### 5. Keep Digital Copies
Photos of your passport, tickets, and hotel bookings on your phone — just in case.

## What If I'm Not Eligible?

If your country isn't on the list, you'll need a regular visa:

- **Tourist visa (L visa):** Standard for tourism
- **Business visa (M visa):** For business activities
- **Transit visa (G visa):** For short transits

Apply at your nearest Chinese embassy or consulate. Processing takes 4-7 business days.
