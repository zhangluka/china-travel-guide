# 🛡️ China Travel Insurance 2026

Do you need travel insurance for China? Short answer: **yes, strongly recommended.** Here's why and how to get it.

## Do I Need Travel Insurance for China?

China doesn't require travel insurance for entry, but you should have it because:

1. **Medical care is expensive for foreigners** — a hospital visit can cost ¥5,000-50,000+ without insurance
2. **Language barrier** — navigating the Chinese healthcare system without help is stressful
3. **Trip disruptions** — train cancellations, flight delays, and weather events are common
4. **Lost belongings** — pickpocketing is rare but possible in crowded tourist areas
5. **Adventure activities** — hiking, glass bridges, and Huashan plank walk carry real risks

## China-Specific Considerations

### Medical Care in China
- **Public hospitals**: Cheap but chaotic, no English, long waits
- **International hospitals**: Expensive but English-speaking, in major cities (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu)
- **Pharmacies**: Widely available, many medications available without prescription
- **Ambulance**: Call 120 (Chinese-speaking) or ask your hotel to call

### Common Tourist Health Issues
- **Food-related stomach issues** — most common complaint. Usually mild, lasts 1-2 days.
- **Air pollution** — can affect those with respiratory conditions. Check AQI daily.
- **Heat stroke** — summer temperatures in some cities exceed 40°C
- **Altitude sickness** — if visiting Tibet or western Sichuan (2,500m+)

### What to Bring (Just in Case)
- Basic medication (anti-diarrhea, painkillers, antihistamines)
- Copies of prescriptions for any regular medication
- Travel insurance policy number and emergency hotline
- Your blood type (useful in emergencies)

## Pro Tips

1. **Buy insurance before departure** — most policies must be purchased before you leave home.

2. **Read the fine print** — check exclusions for adventure activities if you're planning to hike or do the Huashan plank walk.

3. **Keep digital copies** — save your policy number, emergency hotline, and claims process as screenshots on your phone.

4. **International hospitals exist** — Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and Shenzhen all have English-speaking international hospitals. Your insurance provider can direct you to one.

5. **Call your insurance before treatment** — if possible, call the 24/7 hotline before going to the hospital. They can arrange direct payment and recommend a facility.

## FAQ

**Is travel insurance mandatory for China?**
No, China doesn't require travel insurance for entry. However, it's strongly recommended due to the high cost of medical care for foreigners.

**How much does China travel insurance cost?**
Budget plans start at $15-30 for a 7-day trip. Mid-range plans cost $30-60. Comprehensive plans with adventure coverage cost $60-120 for a week.

**Does travel insurance cover COVID in China?**
Many policies now cover COVID-related medical expenses and trip disruptions. Check your specific policy for details.

**What happens if I get sick in China without insurance?**
You'll pay out of pocket. A simple hospital visit costs ¥2,000-5,000. Serious issues can cost ¥50,000+. International hospitals are more expensive.

**Should I get insurance for a short trip to China?**
Yes. Even a 3-day trip can be disrupted by illness, weather, or lost belongings. The cost of insurance is minimal compared to potential expenses.
