# 🌃 Shanghai

Shanghai is China&apos;s most international city — a place where the 1920s Art Deco past coexists with a hyper-modern skyline, and where every cuisine on earth is represented. It&apos;s also one of the easiest Chinese cities for foreign tourists: English is more widely spoken here than anywhere else in China, payment is seamless with Alipay/WeChat, and the metro is clean, modern, and actually works.

## Why Shanghai?

Most first-time visitors to China fixate on Beijing. But Shanghai is where China&apos;s future is most visible — and where the friction of travel is lowest for international visitors.

**What makes Shanghai different:**
- **Ultra-modern infrastructure** — the metro, airports, and public services are world-class
- **Genuinely international** — expats, foreign restaurants, English signage everywhere
- **Walkable waterfronts** — The Bund and Huangpu River are iconic
- **Art Deco heritage** — the colonial-era buildings in Xintiandi and the French Concession are gorgeous
- **Food** — from street xiaolongbao to 3-Michelin-star restaurants

## Who is Shanghai for?

- First-time visitors who want an &quot;easy&quot; Chinese city
- Business travelers with a day or two to explore
- Culture lovers (museums, galleries, theater)
- Nightlife seekers (Shanghai&apos;s bar scene is excellent)
- Foodies (Shanghainese cuisine + global cuisines)

## Best Time to Visit

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Spring (Mar–May)** | Pleasant weather, flowers blooming | Can be rainy |
| **Autumn (Sep–Nov)** | Best weather, comfortable temps | Golden Week (Oct 1–7) is chaotic |
| **Summer (Jun–Aug)** | Hot (30–35°C), humid, typhoons possible | Not ideal |
| **Winter (Dec–Feb)** | Cold but quiet, Christmas decorations | No outdoor appeal |

## Practical Notes

- **English**: Widely understood in tourist areas, hotels, restaurants. You can get by with English here more than any other Chinese city.
- **Payment**: Alipay and WeChat Pay work perfectly. Some hotels accept foreign cards at the front desk, but carry Alipay for everything else.
- **Metro**: The Shanghai Metro is one of the best in the world. Get a Shanghai Public Transportation Card (or use your phone&apos;s transit card) for seamless metro and bus travel.
- **VPN**: Still needed for Google, YouTube, WhatsApp. Set up before you arrive.
- **Air quality**: Can be poor. Check the AQI before planning outdoor activities.
