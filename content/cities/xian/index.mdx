# ⚔️ Xi'an — Where Ancient China Lives

Xi'an (西安, Xī'ān) was the capital of China for 13 dynasties and the starting point of the Silk Road. It's home to the world-famous Terracotta Warriors, the best street food in China, and a 600-year-old city wall you can bike on. If you want to understand China's history, start here.

## Why Visit Xi'an?

- **⚔️ Terracotta Warriors**: One of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century — 8,000 life-size soldiers buried for 2,200 years
- **🍜 Street Food Capital**: The Muslim Quarter has the best street food scene in all of China
- **🏯 Ancient City Wall**: The most complete ancient city wall in China — bike the full 14km loop
- **⛰️ Mount Huashan**: One of China's five sacred mountains, with the world's most dangerous hike
- **🕌 Muslim Quarter**: 1,300 years of Islamic culture in the heart of China
- **💰 Affordable**: Much cheaper than Beijing or Shanghai

## Getting There

### By Air
**Xi'an Xianyang International Airport (XIY)**
- Direct flights from many Asian cities, some European cities
- Airport to city center: Airport Metro (~40 min, ¥9) or taxi (~¥120-150)

### By Train
- **High-speed rail** from Beijing (4.5 hours), Shanghai (6 hours), Chengdu (3 hours), Chongqing (5 hours)
- Xi'an North Station (西安北站) is the main high-speed rail hub
- Book via [Trip.com](https://www.trip.com) (English) or 12306 (Chinese)

### By Train from Chengdu
The **Chengdu-Xi'an high-speed rail** is a popular route:
- ~3 hours, ¥260-400 depending on class
- Scenic route through the Qinling Mountains

## Getting Around Xi'an

Xi'an's city center is flat and compact — much easier to navigate than mountain cities like Chongqing.

- **Metro**: 8+ lines covering most tourist areas, ¥2-8 per trip
- **Didi (ride-hailing)**: ¥10-40 for most city trips
- **Bus**: ¥2, but confusing for non-Chinese speakers
- **Taxi**: ¥9 starting fare, ¥2/km after
- **Bike rental**: Great for the city wall and flat city center

## What to Eat

Xi'an has the **best street food in China**. The Muslim Quarter alone has hundreds of stalls and restaurants.

**Must-try dishes:**
- 🫓 **Roujiamo (肉夹馍)** — "Chinese hamburger": slow-cooked meat in a crispy flatbread
- 🍜 **Biangbiang noodles (biángbiáng面)** — Belt-wide noodles with chili oil and vinegar
- 🥘 **Yangrou paomo (羊肉泡馍)** — Lamb soup with torn bread — Xi'an's signature dish
- 🍢 **Kabob skewers (烤肉)** — Muslim Quarter lamb skewers with cumin
- 🫕 **Hot and sour noodles (酸辣粉)** — Glass noodles in spicy vinegar broth
- 🥮 **Persimmon cake (柿子饼)** — Fried sweet persimmon dessert

See our [Xi'an Food Guide](/city/xian/xian-food-guide/).

## Safety

Xi'an is **very safe** for foreign tourists:
- Violent crime against tourists is extremely rare
- The Muslim Quarter is crowded — watch for pickpockets
- Scams at the Terracotta Warriors are common (see our guide)
- Summer heat can be intense — stay hydrated
- Air quality varies — check AQI in winter
