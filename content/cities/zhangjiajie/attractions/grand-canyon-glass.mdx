# 🌉 Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge (张家界大峡谷玻璃桥)

The world's longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge — 430m long, 300m above the canyon floor. A walk across is a once-in-a-lifetime experience.

## Overview

The **Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge** opened in 2016 and immediately became one of China's most viral attractions. It spans the Grand Canyon, suspended 300 meters above the valley floor. The bridge is 430m long and 6m wide, with a transparent glass bottom that lets you see straight down to the river below.

## How to Get There

- **From Zhangjiajie City**: Bus from the bus station (~1.5 hours, ¥20) or taxi (~¥150-200)
- **From Wulingyuan**: Bus (~1 hour, ¥15) or taxi (~¥100-150)
- **Best option**: Hire a car for the day (¥300-400) — covers transport and flexibility

## What to See

### The Glass Bridge ⭐
The main attraction. Walk across 430m of transparent glass with a 300m drop below. Some sections are more transparent than others — the middle is the most thrilling.

**Tips for the bridge:**
- You must wear cloth shoe covers (provided) to protect the glass
- No high heels allowed
- No running or jumping
- Photography is allowed (phones and small cameras)
- No selfie sticks

### The Grand Canyon
After the bridge, you descend into the canyon via a series of stairs and walkways:
- Waterfalls
- Zip line (optional, extra ¥50-100)
- Boat ride on the river at the bottom
- The canyon itself is beautiful — lush vegetation, clear water

### Bungee Jump (Optional)
The world's highest bungee jump from a glass bridge — 260m drop.
- Price: ¥2,998 (yes, really)
- Only for the extremely brave

## Pro Tips

1. **Book tickets 3-5 days in advance** — daily capacity is limited to 8,000 visitors. Sold out on weekends and holidays.

2. **Go early morning** — less crowded, better light for photos.

3. **Combine with the canyon walk** — the bridge-only ticket (¥138) misses the best part. The canyon descent with waterfalls is spectacular.

4. **Don't look down if you're scared** — honestly, the glass is so clean you forget it's there after a few steps. But the middle section is intense.

5. **Bring a waterproof phone case** — the canyon walk has waterfalls and spray.

6. **Allow extra time for transport** — the bridge is 60km from the city. Factor in 1-2 hours each way.

7. **The bridge is not near the National Forest Park** — it's a separate location. Plan accordingly.

## FAQ

**How scary is the Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge?**
Moderately scary. The glass is thick and completely safe, but the 300m drop is visible through the transparent floor. Most people find it thrilling rather than terrifying.

**How much does the Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge cost?**
¥228 for the bridge + canyon combo, ¥138 for bridge-only. Book in advance as daily capacity is limited to 8,000 visitors.

**Is the Zhangjiajie Glass Bridge worth visiting?**
Yes, especially combined with the canyon walk. The bridge itself is a 20-minute experience, but the canyon descent with waterfalls and river views is equally impressive.

**How do I get to the Glass Bridge from Zhangjiajie?**
It's about 60km from the city. Take a bus (1.5 hours, ¥20) or hire a car (¥300-400 round-trip). There's no direct public transport.

**Can you do the Glass Bridge and National Forest Park in one day?**
Not recommended. They're in different locations and each deserves a full day. The bridge + canyon takes half a day including transport.
