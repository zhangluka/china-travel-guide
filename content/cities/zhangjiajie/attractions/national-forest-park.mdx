# 🏔️ Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (张家界国家森林公园)

The quartzite sandstone pillars that inspired James Cameron's Avatar — over 3,000 towering columns rising from misty subtropical forest. One of the most extraordinary landscapes on Earth.

## Overview

**Zhangjiajie National Forest Park** (张家界国家森林公园) was China's first national forest park (1982) and a UNESCO Global Geopark. The park spans 48 square kilometers and contains over 3,000 quartzite sandstone pillars, many over 200 meters tall. The "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" (originally "Southern Sky Column") was officially renamed after the film in 2010.

## How to Get There

- **From Zhangjiajie City**: Bus from Zhangjiajie Bus Station → Wulingyuan (~40 min, ¥12)
- **From Wulingyuan Town**: Walk or taxi to the park entrance (5-10 min)
- **From train station**: Bus or taxi to Wulingyuan (~1 hour, ¥50-80)

## The Four Main Zones

### 1. Yuanjiajie (袁家界) — Avatar Mountains ⭐ MUST-SEE
The iconic pillar formations. This is where "Avatar Hallelujah Mountain" is located.

**Highlights:**
- **Avatar Hallelujah Mountain** (哈利路亚山) — the 1,080m pillar from the film
- **First Bridge Under Heaven** (天下第一桥) — a natural stone arch connecting two pillars
- **Back Garden** (后花园) — a hidden valley of pillars
- **Enchanted Terrace** (迷魂台) — panoramic viewpoint

**Getting there:** Take the Bailong Elevator (百龙天梯, ¥72 one-way) or hike up.

### 2. Golden Whip Stream (金鞭溪) — Valley Walk
A 7.5km stream valley with towering pillars on both sides. Flat, easy walking.

**Highlights:**
- Golden Whip Rock — 380m tall sandstone pillar
- Monkey groups (wild macaques — don't feed them)
- Clean stream water, lush vegetation

**Time:** 2-3 hours one-way

### 3. Tianzi Mountain (天子山) — Panoramic Views
The highest viewpoint area in the park. Stunning sunrise and sea-of-clouds views.

**Highlights:**
- **Imperial Writing Brush Peak** (御笔峰) — the park's most photographed pillar group
- **Fairy Scattering Flowers** (仙女散花) — a pillar shaped like a woman
- **Helong Park** — panoramic viewing platform

**Getting there:** Cable car (¥72 one-way) or shuttle bus

### 4. Yangjiajie (杨家界) — Off the Beaten Path
Less crowded than Yuanjiajie, with equally dramatic scenery.

**Highlights:**
- **Oolong Village** (乌龙寨) — a narrow path through cliff-side passages
- **Natural Great Wall** — a wall-like rock formation
- Fewer tourists, more adventurous trails

## Pro Tips

1. **Buy the 4-day ticket** — ¥225 covers everything including shuttle buses. You can't see it all in one day.

2. **Start at Yuanjiajie** — take the Bailong Elevator up first thing in the morning (opens 8:00 AM). This is the most popular area and gets crowded by 10:00 AM.

3. **Go early** — be at the park entrance by 7:30 AM. The morning mist on the pillars is magical and the crowds are thin.

4. **Wear comfortable shoes** — the park involves lots of walking on uneven stairs. Some trails have thousands of steps.

5. **Bring rain gear** — weather changes quickly in the mountains. A light rain jacket is essential.

6. **Don't feed the monkeys** — they're wild macaques along Golden Whip Stream. They can be aggressive if they think you have food.

7. **Avoid weekends and holidays** — the park gets extremely crowded during Chinese holidays. Weekday mornings are best.

8. **Stay in Wulingyuan Town** — it's right at the park entrance. Saves 1+ hour of commuting each day.

## FAQ

**How long do you need at Zhangjiajie National Forest Park?**
2-3 days is ideal. You can see highlights in 1 long day, but you'll be rushing and miss the best morning light. The 4-day ticket is designed for multi-day visits.

**How much does Zhangjiajie National Forest Park cost?**
¥225 for a 4-day ticket (includes shuttle buses). Add ¥72 each for the Bailong Elevator and Tianzi Mountain cable car (one-way).

**Is the Zhangjiajie Avatar Mountains worth visiting?**
Absolutely. The landscape is genuinely otherworldly — nothing else on Earth looks like it. The morning mist rising through the pillars is one of the most photographed scenes in China.

**What is the best time to visit Zhangjiajie National Forest Park?**
Early morning (8:00-10:00 AM) for misty pillar views. April-June for spring mist, September-November for autumn colors. Avoid July-August (crowded + hot).

**How do I get to Zhangjiajie National Forest Park?**
Take a bus from Zhangjiajie Bus Station to Wulingyuan (~40 min, ¥12). Stay in Wulingyuan Town, which is walking distance from the park entrance.
