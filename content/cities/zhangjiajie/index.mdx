# 🏔️ Zhangjiajie — The Avatar Mountains

Zhangjiajie (张家界, Zhāngjiājiè) is home to the pillar-like sandstone mountains that inspired the floating Hallelujah Mountains in James Cameron's Avatar. It's one of the most visually stunning landscapes in China — and on Earth.

## Why Visit Zhangjiajie?

- **🏔️ Avatar Mountains**: The quartzite sandstone pillars that inspired the film — over 3,000 of them rising from misty forests
- **🌉 Glass Bridge**: The world's longest and highest glass-bottomed bridge spanning a 300m-deep canyon
- **⛩️ Tianmen Mountain**: A 1,518m mountain with a natural arch, cliff-hanging walkway, and the world's longest cable car ride
- **🌿 National Forest Park**: UNESCO Global Geopark with incredible biodiversity
- **📸 Photography paradise**: Every view is a postcard — misty mornings are otherworldly

## Getting There

### By Air
**Zhangjiajie Hehua International Airport (DYG)**
- Domestic flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu (2-3 hours)
- Limited international flights
- Airport to city center: taxi ~¥30-50, ~20 minutes

### By Train
- **High-speed rail** from Changsha (3 hours), Guangzhou (4 hours), Beijing (7 hours)
- Zhangjiajie West Station (张家界西站) is the main hub
- Book via [Trip.com](https://www.trip.com) (English) or 12306 (Chinese)

### From Changsha (Most Common Route)
Many visitors combine Zhangjiajie with Changsha:
- High-speed train: Changsha → Zhangjiajie West (~3 hours, ¥150)
- Or fly: Changsha → Zhangjiajie (~1 hour)

## Getting Around Zhangjiajie

- **Taxi/Didi**: ¥10-30 for city trips
- **Bus**: To scenic areas from the bus station (¥10-20)
- **Scenic area shuttle**: Included in park tickets, connects different zones
- **Cable cars**: At Wulingyuan and Tianmen Mountain

## Where to Stay

| Area | Vibe | Price Range |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Zhangjiajie City** | Near Tianmen Mountain, transport hub | ¥150-400/night |
| **Wulingyuan Town** | Near the National Forest Park entrance | ¥150-400/night |
| **Near Glass Bridge** | Remote, quiet, close to Grand Canyon | ¥100-300/night |

**Recommendation**: Stay in **Wulingyuan Town** if visiting the National Forest Park (most visitors do). Stay in **Zhangjiajie City** if visiting Tianmen Mountain.

## What to Eat

Zhangjiajie cuisine is Hunan-style — spicy and flavorful.

**Must-try:**
- 🌶️ **Stinky Tofu (臭豆腐)** — Changsha's famous snack, available everywhere
- 🍖 **Tujia Smoked Meat (土家腊肉)** — Smoked bacon, a local Tujia ethnic specialty
- 🍜 **Rice Noodles (米粉)** — Spicy rice noodles for breakfast
- 🫕 **Sour Fish Soup (酸鱼汤)** — A Tujia dish, sour and spicy
- 🥬 **Wild Vegetables** — Foraged greens from the mountains

## Safety

Zhangjiajie is **very safe** for tourists:
- The main risk is physical — lots of walking, steep stairs, and high altitudes
- Glass bridge and cliff walkways are safe but not for those with severe acrophobia
- Weather changes quickly in the mountains — bring rain gear
- Scams exist at the train station — ignore touts offering "cheap tours"
