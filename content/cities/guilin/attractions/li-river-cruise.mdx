# 🛶 Li River Cruise (漓江)

The iconic 4-5 hour cruise from Guilin to Yangshuo through one of the most beautiful landscapes on Earth — limestone karst mountains rising from the misty river.

## Overview

The **Li River Cruise** (漓江, Lí Jiāng) is one of China's most famous scenic experiences. The 83km stretch from Guilin to Yangshuo passes through a landscape of dramatic limestone karst peaks, bamboo groves, water buffalo, and fishing villages. The scenery appears on the ¥20 banknote.

## Essential Info

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Route** | Guilin (Zhujiang Wharf) → Yangshuo |
| **Duration** | 4-5 hours |
| **Ticket price** | ¥216-320 (depending on boat type) |
| **Departure** | Usually 9:00-10:00 AM |
| **Best time** | April-October (April-May for mist, September-October for clarity) |

## How to Book

### Option 1: Hotel/Hostel (Recommended)
- Most hotels in Guilin and Yangshuo can book cruise tickets
- Often includes transport to the wharf
- Price: ¥216-320

### Option 2: Trip.com
- Book online in English
- Includes pickup from hotel
- Price: ¥250-350

### Option 3: Direct at the Wharf
- Buy tickets at Zhujiang Wharf (竹江码头)
- Less convenient — you need to get to the wharf yourself

## What to See

### The Scenery
- **Elephant Trunk Hill** (象鼻山) — Guilin's iconic landmark, elephant-shaped rock
- **Pagoda Hill** — twin pagodas on a hilltop
- **Half-Side Ferry** (半边渡) — a cliff that forces the river to make a sharp turn
- **Mural Hill** (画山) — cliff face with natural patterns resembling a painting
- **Yellow Cloth Shoal** (黄布倒影) — the scene on the ¥20 banknote
- **Xingping** (兴坪) — ancient town with the most photographed viewpoint

### On the Boat
- **Upper deck**: Outdoor seating, best views, more expensive
- **Lower deck**: Indoor seating, air-conditioned, cheaper
- **Snacks and drinks**: Available for purchase on board
- **Bathrooms**: Available on all boats

## Pro Tips

1. **Book the morning departure** — the light is best and the river is calmer. Afternoon cruises can be hazy.

2. **Sit on the right side (going downstream)** — most of the best scenery is on the right bank.

3. **Upper deck is worth it** — the extra ¥50-100 gets you unobstructed views and photo opportunities.

4. **Bring sunscreen and a hat** — there's no shade on the upper deck.

5. **Don't expect silence** — the boats are large (100+ passengers) and there's usually a guide on loudspeaker. Enjoy the scenery and tune out the noise.

6. **Combine with Xingping** — the small town of Xingping (兴坪) has the best viewpoints. Consider stopping there instead of going all the way to Yangshuo.

7. **The bamboo raft option exists** — for a more intimate experience, take a bamboo raft on the Yulong River (遇龙河) near Yangshuo instead.

## FAQ

**How long is the Li River Cruise?**
4-5 hours from Guilin to Yangshuo, covering 83km of river.

**How much does the Li River Cruise cost?**
¥216-320 depending on the boat type and class. Upper deck costs more than lower deck.

**Is the Li River Cruise worth it?**
Yes, it's one of China's most iconic experiences. The karst mountain scenery is genuinely breathtaking, especially in the morning mist.

**What is the best time to do the Li River Cruise?**
April-October for the best weather and water levels. Morning departures have the best light. April-May offers misty, atmospheric views.

**How do I book the Li River Cruise?**
Through your hotel/hostel (easiest), Trip.com (English), or directly at Zhujiang Wharf. Booking through your hotel is recommended.

**Can I do the Li River Cruise as a day trip from Guilin?**
Yes, most visitors do. Take the morning cruise to Yangshuo, explore Yangshuo in the afternoon, and return to Guilin by bus in the evening.
