# ⛰️ Guilin — China's Most Beautiful Landscape

Guilin (桂林, Guìlín) is famous for having the most beautiful natural scenery in China. The karst mountains rising from the Li River, the rice terraces of Longji, and the laid-back town of Yangshuo make this one of the most photogenic destinations in Asia.

## Why Visit Guilin?

- **🛶 Li River Cruise**: The iconic 4-hour cruise through karst mountains — one of China's most photographed scenes
- **🚲 Yangshuo Cycling**: Bike through rice paddies, riverside villages, and karst peaks
- **🌾 Longji Rice Terraces**: 650-year-old rice terraces carved into mountainsides — spectacular in any season
- **🏔️ Karst Mountains**: Thousands of limestone peaks rising from flat plains — unlike anywhere else on Earth
- **🌍 International Tourist Hub**: More English spoken here than most Chinese cities — easier for first-timers

## Getting There

### By Air
**Guilin Liangjiang International Airport (KWL)**
- Domestic flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu (2-3 hours)
- Limited international flights
- Airport to city center: taxi ~¥100-120, ~40 minutes

### By Train
- **High-speed rail** from Guangzhou (3 hours), Shenzhen (3.5 hours), Changsha (3 hours)
- Guilin North Station (桂林北站) is the main hub
- Book via [Trip.com](https://www.trip.com) (English) or 12306 (Chinese)

### From Hong Kong/Shenzhen
- High-speed train: Shenzhen → Guilin (~3.5 hours, ¥200)
- Or fly from Hong Kong (~1.5 hours)

## Getting Around Guilin

- **Taxi/Didi**: ¥10-30 for city trips
- **Bus**: ¥1-2 per trip
- **Bicycle rental**: Great for Yangshuo (¥20-50/day)
- **Scooter rental**: Popular in Yangshuo (¥80-150/day)

## Where to Stay

| Area | Vibe | Price Range |
|------|------|-------------|
| **Guilin City Center** | Near Two Rivers & Four Lakes, transport hub | ¥150-400/night |
| **Yangshuo** | Backpacker hub, cycling, relaxed | ¥100-350/night |
| **Longji Rice Terraces** | Homestays among the terraces | ¥100-250/night |

**Recommendation**: Stay in **Yangshuo** for the best experience. It's the tourist capital of the area with restaurants, bars, and easy access to cycling routes.

## What to Eat

Guilin cuisine is mild compared to Sichuan — fresh, light, and rice-noodle-heavy.

**Must-try:**
- 🍜 **Guilin Rice Noodles (桂林米粉)** — The city's soul food: rice noodles with pickled beans and gravy
- 🐟 **Beer Fish (啤酒鱼)** — Yangshuo's signature dish: whole fish cooked in beer sauce
- 🥘 **Stuffed Li River Snails (酿螺)** — Snails stuffed with minced meat and herbs
- 🍖 **Guilin Mifen (卤粉)** — Another rice noodle variant with braised meat
- 🥚 **Tea Eggs (茶叶蛋)** — Eggs boiled in tea and spices, sold everywhere

## Safety

Guilin is **very safe** for tourists:
- One of the most tourist-friendly cities in China
- More English spoken than most Chinese cities
- Petty theft is rare but possible in crowded areas
- Scams targeting tourists exist (see our guides)
- The Li River cruise is very safe
