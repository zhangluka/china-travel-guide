# 🍢 Chengdu Street Food Guide

Chengdu's street food scene is legendary. From night markets to hidden alleys, there's always something delicious being cooked on the street.

## Best Street Food Areas

### Jianshe Road Night Market (建设路夜市)
- **Location**: Jianshe Road, near Electronic Science and Technology University
- **Hours**: 5 PM - midnight
- **Vibe**: University crowd, tons of variety, very local
- **Must-try**: Grilled skewers (烧烤), stinky tofu (臭豆腐), fried potatoes

### Yulin Road (玉林路)
- **Location**: Yulin neighborhood, central Chengdu
- **Hours**: All day (best for dinner)
- **Vibe**: Local residential area, authentic restaurants
- **Must-try**: Yulin串串 (skewer hotpot), local BBQ, rabbit head

### Jinli Ancient Street (锦里)
- **Location**: Next to Wuhou Temple
- **Hours**: 9 AM - 10 PM
- **Vibe**: Touristy but fun, good for trying a variety of things
- **Must-try**: Three Cannon Balls (三大炮), sugar painting, bamboo rice

### Kuixinglou Street (奎星楼街)
- **Location**: Near Kuanzhai Alley
- **Hours**: All day
- **Vibe**: Hipster food street, Instagram-worthy
- **Must-try**: Creative desserts, fusion snacks, craft drinks

## Street Food Tips

- **Follow the crowds** — if a stall has a long line, it's good
- **Watch them cook** — freshly made = safer and tastier
- **Carry cash** — while many stalls accept Alipay, some older ones only take cash
- **Point and smile** — if you can't read the menu, just point at what looks good
- **Start mild** — build up your spice tolerance gradually
- **Bring tissues** — street food is messy and napkins are scarce

## FAQ

**Is Chengdu street food safe to eat?**
Yes, generally. Choose busy stalls with high turnover (fresh food), ensure food is cooked to order in front of you, and avoid raw salads. Hot, freshly cooked street food is safe for most visitors.

**Where is the best street food in Chengdu?**
Jianshe Road Night Market (near the university) for the most local experience, Yulin Road for authentic neighborhood food, and Jinli for a tourist-friendly introduction. For the best local food, follow the crowds.

**How much does street food cost in Chengdu?**
Street food items typically cost ¥8-30 per dish. A full evening of eating at a night market costs approximately ¥50-100.

**Do street food vendors accept Alipay?**
Most do, but carry ¥100-200 in cash as backup — some older vendors only accept cash.

**What street food is best for beginners?**
Try Bo Bo Chicken (冷串串, cold chicken skewers in chili oil), grilled skewers (烧烤), fried potato cakes (锅巴土豆), and Three Cannon Balls (三大炮). These are mild to moderately spicy.

**Is tap water safe to drink in Chengdu?**
No. Use bottled water (widely available, ¥2-5 per bottle) or boil tap water before drinking.
