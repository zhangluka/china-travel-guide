# 📅 Chengdu 3-Day Itinerary

This itinerary covers the essential Chengdu experience: pandas, food, culture, and the famous Chengdu lifestyle.

## Day 1: Pandas & City Center

### Morning (7:30 AM - 12:00 PM)
🐼 **Giant Panda Breeding Research Base**
- Arrive at 7:30 AM (opening time) — pandas are most active in the morning
- Spend 3-4 hours exploring
- Don't miss: Baby Panda Nursery, Red Panda area, Swan Lake
- See our [Panda Base Guide](/city/chengdu/giant-panda-base/)

### Afternoon (12:30 PM - 5:00 PM)
🍜 **Lunch**: Grab noodles near the panda base, or head back to the city
🏮 **Jinli Ancient Street** (锦里)
- Walk the 550m street, try street food
- See traditional architecture and street performers
- Browse souvenir shops
- Adjacent: **Wuhou Temple** (武侯祠) if interested in Three Kingdoms history

### Evening (6:00 PM - 10:00 PM)
🌶️ **Sichuan Hotpot Dinner**
- This is THE Chengdu experience — don't skip it
- Recommended: Haidilao (tourist-friendly) or Dalongyi (authentic)
- See our [Hotpot Guide](/city/chengdu/hotpot-guide/)
🌙 **Night walk at Taikoo Li** — modern Chengdu meets ancient architecture

## Day 2: Culture & Food

### Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
🍵 **Kuanzhai Alley** (宽窄巷子)
- Explore the three alleys
- Sit in a teahouse for traditional covered-bowl tea
- Watch a Sichuan opera face-changing performance

### Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM)
📖 **Du Fu Thatched Cottage** (杜甫草堂)
- Beautiful gardens and the famous Tang Dynasty poet's residence
- ¥50 entry, 1-2 hours

🍽️ **Late Lunch**: Walk to **Kui Xing Lou Street** (奎星楼街) — trendy food street with tons of options

### Evening (6:00 PM - late)
🌶️ **Yulin Road Food Crawl**
- This is where locals actually eat
- Try: Chuanchuan (串串), grilled skewers (烧烤), rabbit head (兔头)
- End with a local craft beer at one of the neighborhood bars
- See our [Street Food Guide](/city/chengdu/street-food/)

## Day 3: Day Trip or Deep Dive

### Option A: Leshan Giant Buddha (Full Day)
🗿 **Leshan Giant Buddha** — the world's largest stone Buddha
- 2 hours from Chengdu by train/bus
- Book high-speed train: Chengdu → Leshan (~1 hour, ¥65)
- The Buddha is 71 meters tall, carved into a cliff face
- Allow 3-4 hours for the visit
- Combine with a seafood lunch in Leshan

### Option B: Dujiangyan + Panda Volunteer (Full Day)
🌊 **Dujiangyan Irrigation System** — 2,000-year-old water engineering marvel
- 30 min by train from Chengdu
- UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Combine with a panda volunteer program at Dujiangyan Panda Base

### Option C: Chengdu Deep Dive (Stay in City)
☕ **Morning**: Explore the **University area** — hipster cafes, bookshops, local life
🍵 **Afternoon**: Visit **Renmin Park** (人民公园) — the most authentic teahouse experience. Watch locals play mahjong, practice tai chi, and just... live.
🛍️ **Evening**: **Chunxi Road** shopping + dinner at a local Sichuan restaurant

## Tips

- **Book the Panda Base 1-3 days in advance** — tickets sell out
- **Eat hotpot on Day 1** — your stomach needs time to adjust
- **Don't over-schedule** — Chengdu's charm is in its slowness. Leave time to just sit in a teahouse.
- **Carry tissues and hand sanitizer** — not all restaurants provide them
- **Download Amap offline** — for navigation when signal is weak
