# 🗿 Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛)

The **Leshan Giant Buddha** is the largest and tallest stone Buddha statue in the world, carved into a cliff face at the confluence of three rivers. At 71 meters (233 feet) tall, it's truly awe-inspiring.

## Essential Info

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Leshan City, ~130km from Chengdu |
| **Height** | 71 meters (233 feet) |
| **Built** | 713-803 AD (took 90 years!) |
| **Ticket** | ¥80 (adults) |
| **Hours** | 7:30 AM - 6:30 PM |
| **Time needed** | 3-4 hours |
| **UNESCO** | World Heritage Site (part of Mount Emei and Leshan Giant Buddha) |

## How to Get There from Chengdu

### Option 1: High-Speed Train (Recommended)
- **From**: Chengdu East Station → Leshan Station
- **Time**: ~1 hour
- **Cost**: ¥65 (2nd class)
- **Then**: Bus or taxi from Leshan Station to the Buddha (~¥20, 20 min)

### Option 2: Bus
- **From**: Xinnanmen Bus Station (新南门汽车站)
- **Time**: ~2.5 hours
- **Cost**: ¥50-60

### Option 3: Guided Tour
- Book on Trip.com or Klook
- Includes transport + ticket + guide
- ¥200-400 per person
- Convenient but less flexible

## What to See

### The Giant Buddha
The main attraction. You can view it from:
1. **Top of the cliff** — look down at the Buddha's head
2. **Stairway down the cliff** — get close to the Buddha's feet (90-degree stairway, steep!)
3. **Boat ride** — see the full Buddha from the river (¥70-100, separate ticket)

### The Surrounding Area
- **Lingyun Temple** — at the top of the cliff
- **Sleeping Buddha** — a natural rock formation that looks like a reclining Buddha
- **Oriental Buddha Park** — Buddhist-themed park nearby

## Tips

- **Go early** — crowds build by midday
- **Wear good shoes** — lots of stairs, some steep
- **The stairway down is intense** — 200+ steep steps carved into the cliff. Not for those with fear of heights.
- **Combine with Leshan food** — Leshan is famous for its own cuisine (different from Chengdu). Try 跷脚牛肉 (Qiaojiao Beef) and 钵钵鸡 (Bo Bo Chicken).
- **Weather matters** — avoid rainy days (stairs get slippery)

## FAQ

**How much does Leshan Giant Buddha tickets cost?**
¥80 for adults. Boat rides to view the Buddha from the river cost an additional ¥70-100 as a separate ticket.

**How do I get to Leshan Giant Buddha from Chengdu?**
Take the high-speed train from Chengdu East Station to Leshan Station (about 1 hour, ¥65 for 2nd class), then a bus or taxi for about 20 minutes (¥20). Buses from Xinnanmen Bus Station take about 2.5 hours for ¥50-60.

**How tall is the Leshan Giant Buddha?**
The Buddha stands at 71 meters (233 feet) tall, making it the largest stone Buddha statue in the world. It was carved between 713-803 AD over a 90-year period.

**Is the Leshan Giant Buddha a UNESCO World Heritage Site?**
Yes. The Leshan Giant Buddha is part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site "Mount Emei and Leshan Giant Buddha," inscribed in 1996.

**Is the Leshan Giant Buddha suitable for children?**
Yes, though parents should note the 200+ steep stairs carved into the cliff face. Children under 3 feet may not be allowed on certain sections due to safety concerns.

**What is the best time to visit Leshan Giant Buddha?**
Go early in the morning (before 9 AM) to avoid crowds. Avoid rainy days as the stairs become slippery. Spring and autumn offer the best weather.
