# 🏮 Jinli Ancient Street (锦里古街)

## Overview

Jinli is a 550-meter pedestrian street next to Wuhou Temple, designed to recreate the look and feel of ancient Chengdu during the Three Kingdoms period. It's touristy, yes — but also genuinely atmospheric, especially at night when the red lanterns are lit.

## Essential Info

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Next to Wuhou Temple, Wuhou District |
| **Hours** | Open 24 hours (shops ~9am-10pm) |
| **Cost** | Free to enter |
| **Time needed** | 1-2 hours |
| **Metro** | Line 3, Gaoshengqiao Station (高升桥站), 10-min walk |

## What to Do

### Street Food 🍢
Jinli is one of the best places to try Chengdu street food:
- **Bo Bo Chicken** (钵钵鸡) — cold chicken in chili oil, ¥10-15
- **Three Cannon Balls** (三大炮) — glutinous rice balls, ¥10
- **Sugar Painting** (糖画) — traditional sugar art, ¥10-20
- **Rabbit Head** (兔头) — if you're brave, ¥15-20
- **Bamboo Rice** (竹筒饭) — rice cooked in bamboo, ¥10-15

### Souvenir Shopping 🎁
- Sichuan opera face masks
- Hand-painted fans
- Tea sets and local tea
- Panda-themed everything
- Shu embroidery (蜀绣)

### Night Views 🌙
Jinli is most beautiful after dark when thousands of red lanterns illuminate the street. Come around 7-8 PM for the best atmosphere.

## Tips

- **It IS touristy** — but that's okay, it's still worth a visit
- **Prices are inflated** — street food is 20-30% more expensive than elsewhere
- **Combine with Wuhou Temple** — they're right next to each other
- **Better food nearby** — for authentic local food, walk 10 minutes to the surrounding neighborhoods
- **Photo spots** — the bridge at the entrance and the lantern-lit alleys are the most photogenic

## Nearby

- **Wuhou Temple** (武侯祠) — ¥50, dedicated to the Three Kingdoms era
- **Kuanzhai Alley** — 15-minute walk, more refined atmosphere
- **Yulin Road** (玉林路) — 10-minute walk, where locals actually eat (see our [Food Guide](/city/chengdu/sichuan-food-guide/))
