# 🏮 Jinli Ancient Street (锦里古街)

## Overview

Jinli is a 550-meter pedestrian street next to Wuhou Temple, designed to recreate the look and feel of ancient Chengdu during the Three Kingdoms period. It's touristy, yes — but also genuinely atmospheric, especially at night when the red lanterns are lit.

## Essential Info

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Next to Wuhou Temple, Wuhou District |
| **Hours** | Open 24 hours (shops ~9am-10pm) |
| **Cost** | Free to enter |
| **Time needed** | 1-2 hours |
| **Metro** | Line 3, Gaoshengqiao Station (高升桥站), 10-min walk |

## What to Do

### Street Food 🍢
Jinli is one of the best places to try Chengdu street food:
- **Bo Bo Chicken** (钵钵鸡) — cold chicken in chili oil, ¥10-15
- **Three Cannon Balls** (三大炮) — glutinous rice balls, ¥10
- **Sugar Painting** (糖画) — traditional sugar art, ¥10-20
- **Rabbit Head** (兔头) — if you're brave, ¥15-20
- **Bamboo Rice** (竹筒饭) — rice cooked in bamboo, ¥10-15

### Souvenir Shopping 🎁
- Sichuan opera face masks
- Hand-painted fans
- Tea sets and local tea
- Panda-themed everything
- Shu embroidery (蜀绣)

### Night Views 🌙
Jinli is most beautiful after dark when thousands of red lanterns illuminate the street. Come around 7-8 PM for the best atmosphere.

## Tips

- **It IS touristy** — but that's okay, it's still worth a visit
- **Prices are inflated** — street food is 20-30% more expensive than elsewhere
- **Combine with Wuhou Temple** — they're right next to each other
- **Better food nearby** — for authentic local food, walk 10 minutes to the surrounding neighborhoods
- **Photo spots** — the bridge at the entrance and the lantern-lit alleys are the most photogenic

## FAQ

**Is Jinli Ancient Street free to enter?**
Yes, Jinli Street itself is completely free to enter. You only pay for food, souvenirs, and any activities you choose.

**How late is Jinli Ancient Street open?**
Jinli is open 24 hours, though most shops operate from approximately 9 AM to 10 PM. The red lanterns make it most atmospheric after dark, around 7-8 PM.

**Is Jinli Ancient Street authentic or just for tourists?**
Jinli is touristy but authentic in its architecture and cultural recreation of ancient Shu Kingdom aesthetics. The street food is overpriced compared to local neighborhoods, but it's a genuine historical pedestrian street with real cultural significance.

**How do I get to Jinli from Chengdu city center?**
Take Metro Line 3 to Gaoshengqiao Station (高升桥站), then walk about 10 minutes. Jinli is adjacent to Wuhou Temple.

**What is the famous Red Wall at Jinli/Wuhou Temple?**
The Red Wall (红墙) is a famous photography spot at Wuhou Temple — a long corridor of red walls with bamboo shadows. It's adjacent to Jinli and costs ¥50 to enter Wuhou Temple separately.

**What street food should I try at Jinli?**
Must-try items include Bo Bo Chicken (¥10-15), Three Cannon Balls (¥10), Sugar Painting (¥10-20), and Rabbit Head (¥15-20) if you're adventurous.
