# 🐼 Giant Panda Breeding Research Base

This is THE reason most tourists come to Chengdu — and it's absolutely worth it.

## Overview

The **Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding** (成都大熊猫繁育研究基地) is the world's premier panda conservation facility. It houses over 200 giant pandas and is the best place in the world to see pandas up close in a semi-natural habitat.

## Essential Info

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Address** | 成都市成华区外北熊猫大道1375号 |
| **Opening hours** | 7:30 AM - 6:00 PM (last entry 5:00 PM) |
| **Ticket price** | ¥55 (adults), ¥27 (students/seniors) |
| **Booking** | Book online in advance via WeChat mini-program or official website |
| **Time needed** | 3-5 hours |
| **Best time to visit** | Early morning (7:30-10:00 AM) when pandas are most active |

## How to Get There

- **Metro + Shuttle**: Take Metro Line 3 to Panda Base Station (熊猫大道站), then take the free shuttle bus
- **Didi/Taxi**: ~¥40-60 from city center, ~30 minutes
- **Bus**: Bus 87 or 198 from various city locations

## What to See

### Giant Pandas 🐼
The main attraction. Pandas are most active in the morning (eating bamboo) and tend to sleep in the afternoon. Arrive early!

### Baby Pandas 👶
The nursery house is where you might see baby pandas (born July-September). This is incredibly popular — expect crowds.

### Red Pandas 🔴
Often overlooked, red pandas are adorable and much more active than giant pandas. They roam freely in certain areas.

### Swan Lake 🦢
A beautiful lake area at the back of the base, great for a peaceful walk.

## Tickets & Booking

> ⚠️ **Important**: Tickets MUST be booked in advance. Walk-in tickets are often unavailable, especially during holidays and weekends.

**How to book:**
1. WeChat mini-program: Search "成都大熊猫繁育研究基地"
2. Official website: [panda.org.cn](http://www.panda.org.cn)
3. Third-party: Trip.com, Klook (may have markup)

**Booking tips:**
- Book 1-3 days in advance
- Morning slots sell out fastest
- Bring your passport (required for entry)

## Pro Tips

1. **Arrive at 7:30 AM** — the moment gates open. Pandas eat breakfast and are super active. By noon, they're mostly sleeping.

2. **Go to the back of the base first** — most tourists start at the front and get stuck in crowds. Walk to the far end (Sun Nursery, Activity Room) and work your way back.

3. **Skip the electric cart** — the base is walkable (2-3 km) and the walking paths are beautiful. The cart saves time but you miss things.

4. **Bring snacks and water** — there are restaurants inside but they're overpriced and mediocre.

5. **Wear comfortable shoes** — lots of walking on hilly terrain.

6. **No flash photography** — it disturbs the pandas. Turn off your flash.

7. **Weekdays are less crowded** — avoid weekends and Chinese holidays.

## Nearby: Dujiangyan Panda Base

If you want a more intimate panda experience, consider the **Dujiangyan Panda Base** (都江堰熊猫乐园):
- ~1 hour from Chengdu city
- Much fewer tourists
- You can do a **volunteer program** (clean enclosures, prepare food) for ~¥500
- Combine with a visit to Dujiangyan Irrigation System
