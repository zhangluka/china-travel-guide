# 🌊 Dujiangyan Irrigation System (都江堰)

**Dujiangyan** is an ancient irrigation system built around 256 BC that still functions today — making it one of the oldest and most remarkable engineering projects in human history. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a great half-day or full-day trip from Chengdu.

## How to Get There

- **High-speed train**: Chengdu → Dujiangyan Station, ~30 min, ¥10
- **Then**: Bus or taxi to the scenic area (~15 min, ¥10)
- **Or**: Didi from Chengdu, ~1 hour, ¥150-200

## What to See

### The Three Main Structures

1. **Yuzui (Fish Mouth)** — the dam that splits the river into two channels
2. **Feisha Dam (Flying Sand Dam)** — diverts excess water and sediment
3. **Baopingkou (Bottle-Neck Channel)** — controls water flow into the irrigation system

### The Experience
- Walk across the **Anlan Suspension Bridge** — scenic, slightly wobbly
- Climb to **Erwang Temple** — overlooking the entire system
- The surrounding park is beautiful — lush greenery, ancient trees
- **Hire a guide** — the history is fascinating but hard to appreciate without context. ¥100-200 for a local guide.

## FAQ

**When was Dujiangyan built?**
Dujiangyan was built around 256 BC during the Qin Dynasty by Li Bing, making it over 2,200 years old. It is the world's oldest functioning irrigation system and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

**How much does Dujiangyan ticket cost?**
¥80 for adults. There may be additional fees for specific areas within the scenic zone.

**How do I get to Dujiangyan from Chengdu?**
Take the high-speed train from Chengdu to Dujiangyan Station (about 30 minutes, ¥10), then a bus or taxi for approximately 15 minutes (¥10). Alternatively, take a Didi for about 1 hour (¥150-200).

**Is Dujiangyan suitable for children?**
Yes, children generally enjoy the scenic park environment, the suspension bridge, and the beautiful landscapes. The walking paths are relatively gentle.

**Can I combine Dujiangyan with a panda visit in one day?**
Yes. Dujiangyan Panda Base is approximately 15 minutes from the main Dujiangyan scenic area. You can visit both in one day, though it requires an early start.

**What makes Dujiangyan a UNESCO World Heritage Site?**
Dujiangyan was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2000. Its ingenious design — using natural topography to control water flow without a dam — is considered one of the greatest engineering achievements in human history.
