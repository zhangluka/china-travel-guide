# 🏨 Where to Stay in Chengdu

## Best Areas for Tourists

### 1. Chunxi Road / Taikoo Li (春熙路/太古里) ⭐ Top Pick

**The heart of Chengdu.** Modern shopping district with the best location for sightseeing.

- **Pros**: Central, walkable to many attractions, tons of restaurants, great metro access
- **Cons**: Can be noisy, touristy
- **Budget**: ¥200-300 (hostels), ¥400-800 (hotels)
- **Recommended for**: First-time visitors, shoppers, nightlife lovers

### 2. Kuanzhai Alley Area (宽窄巷子)

**Traditional Chengdu vibes.** Near the famous alleys and more cultural attractions.

- **Pros**: Atmospheric, great teahouses, walkable to Kuanzhai Alley and Du Fu Thatched Cottage
- **Cons**: Slightly less central, fewer modern amenities
- **Budget**: ¥250-600 (boutique hotels)
- **Recommended for**: Culture seekers, tea lovers, photographers

### 3. Jinli / Wuhou District (锦里/武侯区)

**Tourist-friendly zone.** Near Jinli Street and Wuhou Temple.

- **Pros**: Walking distance to Jinli and Wuhou Temple, many tourist services
- **Cons**: Very touristy, food is overpriced
- **Budget**: ¥200-500
- **Recommended for**: Short stays, convenience seekers

### 4. University / Tangba Street (大学路/镗钯街)

**Hipster and local.** The most "authentic Chengdu" neighborhood.

- **Pros**: Best local cafes and restaurants, young vibe, more affordable
- **Cons**: Further from main tourist sites, fewer hotels
- **Budget**: ¥150-400
- **Recommended for**: Repeat visitors, foodies, digital nomads

## Booking Tips

- **Book on Trip.com or Booking.com** — both work well in Chengdu
- **Check hotel policy**: Some budget hotels don't accept foreign guests (must be "涉外" — licensed for foreigners). Mid-range and up are always fine.
- **Passport required**: All hotels require passport registration
- **Check-in time**: Usually 2:00 PM, check-out 12:00 PM
- **Payment**: Most accept Alipay/WeChat. International credit cards accepted at higher-end hotels.
