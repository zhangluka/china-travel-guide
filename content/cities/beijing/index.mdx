# 🏯 Beijing

Beijing is China&apos;s capital and cultural heart — a city where 1,000 years of imperial history sits alongside a hyper-modern metropolis. It&apos;s the place every foreign traveler imagines when they think of China: the Forbidden City, the Great Wall, Tiananmen Square, the Temple of Heaven. But Beijing is also a city of profound subtleties — the hutong neighborhoods, the centuries-old tea houses, the quiet courtyards behind unmarked doors.

If Shanghai is China&apos;s future, Beijing is China&apos;s soul.

## Why Beijing?

Beijing offers something no other Chinese city can: **history at scale**. The Forbidden City alone is larger than most cities. The Temple of Heaven is a masterpiece of Ming dynasty architecture. The Summer Palace is a lakeside escape that took 1,000 artisans 15 years to build. And the Great Wall — the most recognizable structure on earth — is within day-trip distance.

**What makes Beijing different:**
- **Imperial history** — the most concentrated heritage sites in China
- **The Great Wall** — 1.5–2 hours from the city
- **Hutong culture** — ancient alleyways, courtyard homes, local life
- **Arts & culture** — world-class museums, galleries, and performance spaces
- **Peking duck** — the definitive version is in Beijing

## Who is Beijing for?

- First-time visitors to China who want the &quot;classic&quot; Chinese experience
- History and architecture enthusiasts
- Culture lovers (opera, art, traditional medicine)
- Great Wall pilgrims
- Foodies (Peking duck, Imperial cuisine, Beijing&apos;s best regional cuisines)

## Best Time to Visit

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Spring (Apr–Jun)** | Pleasant weather, flowers at Temple of Heaven | Sandstorms possible in Mar-Apr |
| **Autumn (Sep–Oct)** | Clear skies, comfortable temps, beautiful foliage | Golden Week (Oct 1–7) is very crowded |
| **Summer (Jul–Aug)** | Long days, warm nights | Hot (35°C+), humid, many closed for renovation |
| **Winter (Nov–Mar)** | Empty sites, dramatic scenery, lowest prices | Cold (-10°C at night), some sites closed |

## Practical Notes

- **English**: Less widely spoken than Shanghai. Learn a few basic Mandarin phrases or use a translation app.
- **Payment**: Alipay and WeChat Pay are essential — Beijing is more cash-dependent than Shanghai, but mobile payment still works everywhere tourist-oriented.
- **Air quality**: Can be poor, especially in winter. Check AQI before outdoor activities.
- **Politics**: Some areas near Tiananmen Square have security checkpoints. Carry your passport.
- **VPN**: Essential for Google, YouTube, WhatsApp.
- **Uber/Didi**: Didi works in Beijing. Uber does not.
- **Metro**: Extensive and efficient. Get a Beijing transit card (or use your phone).
