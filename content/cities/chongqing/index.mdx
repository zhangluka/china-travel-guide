# 🏙️ Chongqing — The 8D Mountain City

Chongqing (重庆, Chóngqìng) is China's most mind-bending city. Built on mountains where the Yangtze and Jialing rivers meet, it's a cyberpunk maze of skyscrapers, bridges, and roads that seem to defy physics. Foreign YouTubers call it "the 8D city" — and once you see it, you'll understand why.

## Why Visit Chongqing?

- **🌆 8D Architecture**: Roads on top of buildings, metro through apartment blocks, bridges that go nowhere — the city that broke Google Maps
- **🌶️ Hotpot Capital**: Chongqing invented hotpot. The local version is oilier, spicier, and more intense than Chengdu's
- **🌃 Night Scenery**: One of China's "three great night views" — the skyline at Hongya Cave is jaw-dropping
- **🏔️ Wulong Karst**: UNESCO World Heritage sinkholes and natural bridges, 2.5 hours from the city
- **💰 Affordable**: Significantly cheaper than Beijing or Shanghai
- **📱 Viral on YouTube**: Multiple 10M+ view videos — this is the China travel destination of the moment

## Getting There

### By Air
**Chongqing Jiangbei International Airport (CKG)**
- Terminal 3 is the main terminal (huge, opened 2017)
- Direct flights from many Asian cities, some European cities
- Airport to city center: Metro Line 10 (~40 min, ¥7) or taxi (~¥80-120)

### By Train
- **High-speed rail** from Chengdu (1.5 hours), Xi'an (5 hours), Guiyang (2 hours), Kunming (4 hours)
- Chongqing West Station (重庆西站) and Chongqing North Station (重庆北站) are the main hubs
- Book via [Trip.com](https://www.trip.com) (English) or 12306 (Chinese)

### By Train from Chengdu
The **Chengdu-Chongqing high-speed rail** is one of the most popular routes in China:
- ~1.5 hours, ¥150-260 depending on class
- Trains run every 10-15 minutes during peak hours
- This is the easiest way to combine both cities

## Getting Around Chongqing

Chongqing's terrain makes navigation unique — you'll often need to go "up" or "down" to get anywhere.

- **Metro**: 12+ lines, covers most tourist areas, ¥2-10 per trip
- **Monorail**: Lines 2 and 3 run through buildings and over rivers — the ride itself is an attraction
- **Didi (ride-hailing)**: ¥15-60 for most city trips
- **Taxi**: ¥10 starting fare
- **Elevator/escalator**: Chongqing has the longest outdoor escalator in Asia (凯扶梯, 112m)

## What to Eat

Chongqing is the **birthplace of hotpot**. The food here is intense, oily, and incredibly flavorful.

**Must-try dishes:**
- 🌶️ **Chongqing Hotpot** (火锅) — The original. Oil-based broth, offal-heavy, much spicier than Chengdu
- 🍜 **Chongqing Xiaomian** (重庆小面) — The city's soul food: spicy noodles for breakfast
- 🥟 **Wontons in Chili Oil** (抄手) — Silky wontons drowning in red oil
- 🍖 **Chuanchuan** (串串) — Like hotpot but on skewers
- 🫕 **Grilled Fish** (烤鱼) — Whole fish grilled then simmered in spicy broth
- 🧁 **Bobo Chicken** (钵钵鸡) — Cold skewers in a numbing sauce

See our [Chongqing Hotpot Guide](/city/chongqing/chongqing-hotpot/) and [Street Food Guide](/city/chongqing/chongqing-street-food/).

## Safety

Chongqing is **very safe** for foreign tourists:
- Violent crime against tourists is extremely rare
- The city is hilly — watch your step on steep stairs and uneven paths
- Summer heat (40°C+) is the real danger — stay hydrated
- The food is extremely spicy — build up your tolerance gradually
- Air quality has improved but can be hazy in winter
