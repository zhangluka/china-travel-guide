# 🌶️ Chongqing Hotpot — The Real Deal

Chongqing is where hotpot was born. The local version is oilier, spicier, and more intense than anywhere else in China — including Chengdu.

## Chongqing vs. Chengdu Hotpot

| | Chongqing Hotpot | Chengdu Hotpot |
|---|---|---|
| **Broth base** | Pure beef tallow oil (牛油) | Oil + water blend |
| **Spice level** | Extremely spicy, numbing | Spicy but more balanced |
| **Ingredients** | Offal-heavy (intestines, tripe, brain) | More meat and vegetables |
| **Dipping sauce** | Just sesame oil + garlic | Sesame paste, garlic, cilantro, etc. |
| **Vibe** | Loud, chaotic, local | More tourist-friendly |

**Bottom line:** If you've had hotpot in Chengdu, Chongqing hotpot is a different beast. More intense, more traditional, more local.

## How to Order

### Step 1: Choose Your Broth
- **Red broth (红锅)** — Pure spicy oil. This is the Chongqing way. Warning: it's VERY spicy.
- **Half-and-half (鸳鸯锅)** — Split pot with spicy and mild sides. Tourist-friendly option.
- **Nine-grid pot (九宫格)** — The most traditional Chongqing style. 9 separate compartments in one pot, each with different heat levels.

### Step 2: Order Meat
**Must-orders:**
- 🥩 **Yurong Mixture (肉糜)** — A Chongqing specialty: minced meat mixed with egg, cooked in the broth
- 🫕 **Tripe (毛肚)** — The #1 hotpot ingredient in Chongqing. Dip for exactly 15 seconds ("七上八下" — 7 dips up, 8 dips down)
- 🧠 **Duck Brain (鸭脑花)** — Sounds scary, tastes incredible. Creamy and rich.
- 🥬 **Duck Intestines (鸭肠)** — Cook in 10 seconds, chewy and delicious
- 🐄 **Beef slices (嫩牛肉)** — Thin-sliced beef, cooks in seconds
- 🥬 **Lotus root (藕片)** — Crispy and refreshing between bites of meat

### Step 3: Choose Your Dipping Sauce
In Chongqing, the traditional sauce is simple:
- **Sesame oil (麻油)** + **crushed garlic (蒜泥)** + a little **vinegar**
- That's it. No fancy sauces. The broth does the flavoring.

### Step 4: Cook and Eat
- Use chopsticks to dip ingredients into the boiling broth
- Different items cook for different times (tripe = 15 seconds, vegetables = 2-3 minutes)
- Don't eat directly from the communal pot — use your chopsticks to grab, then dip in your sauce bowl

## Best Hotpot Restaurants in Chongqing

### For Tourists (English-Friendly)
- **Haidilao (海底捞)** — Famous chain, English menu, incredible service (free manicures while you wait). Not the most authentic, but very tourist-friendly.
- **Xiaolongkan (小龙坎)** — Popular chain, good quality, more authentic than Haidilao.

### For the Authentic Experience (No English)
- **Donglai Shun (洞亭火锅)** — Underground hotpot in a literal cave. Very local, very authentic.
- **Pipa Yuan (枇杷园)** — Hilltop restaurant with city views. Local favorite.
- **Zhou Yu Hotpot (周师兄火锅)** — Known for their beef and tripe quality.

> **Pro tip:** If you can't read the Chinese menu, show the waiter a photo of dishes you want, or point at what other tables are eating.

## Pro Tips

1. **Start with half-and-half (鸳鸯)** — unless you're a spice veteran. The full red broth is genuinely painful for beginners.

2. **Don't wear white** — the oil splashes. Seriously.

3. **Order tripe first** — it's the #1 ingredient and sets the tone for the meal.

4. **Sesese oil is your friend** — drink the sesame oil dipping sauce generously. It coats your stomach and helps with the spice.

5. **Eat slowly** — Chongqing hotpot is a 2-3 hour social event, not a quick meal.

6. **Skip lunch if you're eating hotpot for dinner** — you'll want to arrive hungry.

7. **Ask for "wei la" (微辣)** if you want mild — though even "mild" in Chongqing is quite spicy.

8. **Bring tissues** — you will sweat. A lot.

## FAQ

**What's the difference between Chongqing and Chengdu hotpot?**
Chongqing hotpot uses pure beef tallow oil, is spicier, and focuses on offal (tripe, intestines, brain). Chengdu hotpot is more balanced with a wider variety of ingredients and dipping sauces.

**How spicy is Chongqing hotpot?**
Very. The standard red broth is extremely spicy and numbing. Order "half-and-half" (鸳鸯锅) if you're not used to spicy food.

**What should I order at Chongqing hotpot?**
Must-orders: tripe (毛肚, 15 seconds), duck brain (鸭脑花), duck intestines (鸭肠), Yurong mixture (肉糜), and lotus root (藕片).

**How much does hotpot cost in Chongqing?**
¥80-150 per person for a good hotpot meal. Budget options start at ¥50, and high-end restaurants can reach ¥200+.

**Do I need to book hotpot restaurants in Chongqing?**
For popular restaurants on weekends, yes. Use Dianping app to book or queue. Weekday dinners are usually fine without reservations.
