# 🏔️ Guizhou — China's Best-Kept Secret

Guizhou (贵州, Guìzhōu) is China's most underrated province — waterfalls, ancient minority villages, vast karst caves, and landscapes that rival Guilin without the crowds. If you've already done Beijing-Xi'an-Chengdu and want something real, this is it.

## Why Guizhou?

- **🌊 Huangguoshu Waterfall** — China's largest waterfall (77.8m high, 101m wide)
- **🏘️ Minority Villages** — Miao and Dong ethnic villages with wooden architecture and living traditions
- **🕳️ Karst Caves** — Some of the world's largest cave systems
- **🍜 Food** — Sour, spicy, and fermented — completely different from other Chinese cuisines
- **👥 No Crowds** — Far fewer tourists than Yunnan, Guilin, or Sichuan
- **💰 Cheap** — One of China's poorest provinces = very affordable for tourists

## How to Get There

- **Fly**: Guiyang (贵阳) is the capital. Direct flights from Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu (2-3 hours)
- **Train**: High-speed rail from Guangzhou (4 hours), Changsha (3 hours), Kunming (2 hours)
- **From Chengdu**: High-speed train ~3 hours

## What to See

### Huangguoshu Waterfall (黄果树瀑布)
China's largest and most famous waterfall. The thundering water is visible from a distance, and you can walk behind the falls through a cave.
- Location: 150km from Guiyang
- Entry: ¥160
- Time: Half day

### Xijiang Miao Village (西江千户苗寨)
The world's largest Miao ethnic village — over 1,000 traditional wooden houses on a hillside. Stunning at night when thousands of lights illuminate the valley.
- Location: 200km from Guiyang
- Entry: ¥90
- Time: 1-2 days

### Libo Small Seven Holes (荔波小七孔)
A scenic area with turquoise rivers, waterfalls, and natural stone bridges. Often called "China's Jiuzhaigou."
- Location: 300km from Guiyang
- Entry: ¥130
- Time: 1 day

### Zhijin Cave (织金洞)
One of the world's most spectacular caves — massive chambers, stalactites, and underground rivers.
- Location: 150km from Guiyang
- Entry: ¥120
- Time: Half day

## When to Go

| Season | Pros | Cons |
|--------|------|------|
| **Spring (Mar-May)** | Green, comfortable weather | Occasional rain |
| **Summer (Jun-Aug)** | Waterfalls at full power | Hot, humid, rainy season |
| **Autumn (Sep-Nov)** | Best weather, clear skies | Less water in waterfalls |
| **Winter (Dec-Feb)** | No crowds, cheap | Cold, some sites closed |

**Best time**: April-May or September-October

## Pro Tips

1. **Hire a driver** — public transport between villages is infrequent and slow. A driver for 3-4 days costs ¥500-800/day.

2. **Learn basic Mandarin** — English is almost nonexistent outside Guiyang. Even basic Chinese helps enormously.

3. **Try the sour soup** — Guizhou's signature dish is sour soup fish (酸汤鱼). It's incredible.

4. **Stay in village guesthouses** — not hotels. The experience of sleeping in a Miao wooden house is unforgettable.

5. **Guizhou is huge** — distances between attractions are long (200-300km). Don't try to see everything in a few days.

## FAQ

**Is Guizhou worth visiting?**
Yes, especially for experienced China travelers who've already seen the major cities. The minority villages, waterfalls, and caves are spectacular, and the lack of foreign tourists makes it feel authentic.

**How many days do I need in Guizhou?**
5-7 days minimum to see the highlights (Huangguoshu, Xijiang, Libo, Zhijin Cave). Distances between attractions are long.

**Is Guizhou difficult to travel in?**
It's more challenging than Beijing or Shanghai — less English, fewer tourist facilities, and longer distances. But it's very rewarding for adventurous travelers.

**What is Guizhou famous for?**
Huangguoshu Waterfall, Miao and Dong minority villages, spicy and sour cuisine, karst caves, and being one of China's least-visited provinces.

**When is the best time to visit Guizhou?**
April-May or September-October. Summer is rainy and hot, winter is cold and some sites are closed.
