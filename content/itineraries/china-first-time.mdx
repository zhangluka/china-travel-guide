# 🇨🇳 First Time in China — 10-Day Starter Itinerary

Never been to China? This itinerary gives you the perfect introduction: imperial history in Beijing, ancient wonders in Xi'an, and pandas + food in Chengdu. It's designed for first-time visitors who want to see the highlights without feeling overwhelmed.

## The Route

```
Beijing (3 nights) → Xi'an (2 nights) → Chengdu (3 nights) → Depart
```

**Why this route:** All three cities are connected by high-speed rail. You move west, ending in Chengdu (the most relaxed city) where you can decompress before flying home.

---

## Day 1-3: Beijing

### Day 1: Arrive & Imperial Beijing
- **Morning**: Arrive, check in, rest
- **Afternoon**: **Forbidden City** (故宫) — 3-4 hours, book tickets in advance
- **Evening**: Walk through **Jingshan Park** for the best Forbidden City sunset view

### Day 2: The Great Wall
- **Full day**: **Mutianyu Great Wall** (recommended over Badaling — less crowded, more scenic)
- Cable car up, walk the wall, toboggan down
- Return to city for **Peking duck dinner** at Da Dong or Quanjude

### Day 3: Temple of Heaven & Hutongs
- **Morning**: **Temple of Heaven** (天坛) — watch locals doing tai chi
- **Afternoon**: **Hutong alleys** — explore the old Beijing neighborhoods by rickshaw or on foot
- **Evening**: **Wangfujing Night Food Street** for street food

**Beijing Essentials:**
- Stay near **Wangfujing** or **Qianmen** for easy access to sights
- Metro is excellent, signs in English
- Forbidden City tickets MUST be booked online in advance

---

## Day 4-5: Xi'an

### Travel Day (Day 4)
- **Morning**: High-speed train Beijing → Xi'an (~4.5 hours, ¥550)
- **Afternoon**: **Muslim Quarter** food walk — roujiamo, biangbiang noodles, lamb skewers
- **Evening**: **Bell Tower** at night, explore the area

### Day 5: Terracotta Warriors & City Wall
- **Morning**: **Terracotta Warriors** — take Tour Bus 306 from train station, arrive by 8:30 AM
- **Afternoon**: **Ancient City Wall** — rent a bike, ride the 14km loop
- **Evening**: Yangrou paomo (lamb bread soup) dinner

**Xi'an Essentials:**
- Stay near the **Bell Tower** — walking distance to everything
- Book Terracotta Warriors tickets in advance
- The Muslim Quarter is best in the evening

---

## Day 6-8: Chengdu

### Travel Day (Day 6)
- **Morning**: High-speed train Xi'an → Chengdu (~3 hours, ¥260)
- **Afternoon**: **Kuanzhai Alley** — tea houses, Sichuan opera, relaxed vibes
- **Evening**: **Sichuan hotpot** — the essential Chengdu experience

### Day 7: Pandas & Jinli
- **Morning**: **Giant Panda Base** — arrive at 7:30 AM (opening time), 3-4 hours
- **Afternoon**: **Jinli Ancient Street** + **Wuhou Temple**
- **Evening**: Street food crawl on **Yulin Road**

### Day 8: Day Trip or Deep Dive
- **Option A**: **Leshan Giant Buddha** day trip (2 hours each way by train)
- **Option B**: **Dujiangyan** irrigation system + panda volunteer program
- **Option C**: Stay in Chengdu — Renmin Park teahouse, Chunxi Road shopping

**Chengdu Essentials:**
- Book Panda Base tickets 1-3 days in advance
- Hotpot is VERY spicy — order "half-and-half" (鸳鸯锅) for a mild option
- The city is flat and walkable — great for decompressing after Beijing/Xi'an

---

## Day 9-10: Buffer & Departure

### Day 9: Flexible
- Use this as a buffer day — revisit somewhere you loved, or explore at your own pace
- **Morning**: Renmin Park teahouse — the most authentic Chengdu experience
- **Afternoon**: Buy souvenirs at Chunxi Road or Taikoo Li

### Day 10: Departure
- Fly out from Chengdu Tianfu International Airport (TFU)
- Metro Line 18 to the airport (~40 min, ¥10)

## Practical Tips for First-Timers

### Before You Fly
1. **Download a VPN** — Google, WhatsApp, Instagram are all blocked
2. **Set up Alipay** — link your foreign credit card (cash is nearly dead)
3. **Download Amap** (高德地图) — Google Maps doesn't work
4. **Get an eSIM** — buy before departure for instant connectivity
5. **Book key tickets** — Forbidden City, Panda Base, Terracotta Warriors

### While in China
6. **Carry tissues** — not all restaurants provide them
7. **Learn 5 Chinese phrases** — hello, thank you, how much, delicious, where is
8. **Show your phone screen** — when there's a language barrier, show the Chinese text
9. **Don't tip** — tipping is not customary and can cause confusion
10. **Take the train** — China's high-speed rail is the best in the world. Use it.

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Over-scheduling**: Leave buffer time. China is intense — you'll need rest days.
- **Eating hotpot on Day 1**: Your stomach needs time to adjust to the spice.
- **Skipping the VPN setup**: You'll be cut off from Google/WhatsApp without one.
- **Relying on cash**: Most places only accept Alipay/WeChat Pay.
- **Going to Badaling Great Wall**: It's the most crowded. Mutianyu is better.
